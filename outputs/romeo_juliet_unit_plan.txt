================================================================================
ROMEO AND JULIET - 6-WEEK UNIT PLAN
Theater Education Pipeline - Unit Generation Simulation
================================================================================

Unit: Shakespeare (Unit 3)
Text: Romeo and Juliet by William Shakespeare
Duration: 30 Instructional Days (6 Weeks)
Grade Level: 9-10
Daily Class Time: 56 minutes

Structure: Light Trim Version (~20% text reduction)
- Scenes Cut: 4 (teacher summary only)
- Scenes Trimmed: 3 (key lines only)
- Reading Days: 15
- Activity Days: 15
- Balance: 50% reading / 50% differentiated activities

Standards Focus: CA Common Core RL.9-10.1-7, SL.9-10.1-6, W.9-10.3

================================================================================
WEEK 1: ACT 1 - INTRODUCTION AND EXPOSITION
================================================================================
Reading Type: Shared Reading with Annotation
Standards: RL.9-10.1, RL.9-10.4, SL.9-10.1

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 1 (Reading) - Prologue and Act 1, Scene 1: The Street Brawl
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

POWERPOINT (12 content slides):
  Slide 1: Agenda & Journal - "What causes conflicts between groups?"
  Slide 2: Warmup - Iambic Pentameter Walk
  Slide 3: Shakespeare Context - Elizabethan theater, Globe Theatre
  Slide 4: Prologue - "Two households, both alike in dignity"
  Slide 5: Sonnet Structure - 14 lines, rhyme scheme, volta
  Slide 6: Key Vocabulary - civil, mutiny, grudge, foe, piteous
  Slide 7: Act 1.1 Setup - Sampson, Gregory, and the thumb-biting
  Slide 8: The Brawl Escalates - Benvolio vs. Tybalt
  Slide 9: Prince Escalus - Consequences established
  Slide 10: Romeo Introduced - Lovesick for Rosaline
  Slide 11: Character Map - Montagues vs. Capulets
  Slide 12: Exit Ticket Prompt
  Slide 13-16: Activity instructions, warmup details, journal/exit

ACTIVITY (15 min):
  Annotation Practice - Students annotate the Prologue with:
  - Circle unfamiliar words
  - Underline key plot points
  - Star theatrical moments

HANDOUTS:
  - Character Relationship Chart (blank)
  - Prologue Annotation Guide
  - Vocabulary List #1 (10 words)

ASSIGNMENT:
  Complete character chart with Act 1 characters
